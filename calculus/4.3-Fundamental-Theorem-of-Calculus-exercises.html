\(34.\)

  3. Evaluate \(f'(4).\)
  4. Identify the \(x\)-coordinates of any critical points of \(f.\)
  5. If \(g''\) is continuous, then find \(\int_{-6}^4 g''(x) \di x.\)

\(44.\)
Let \(R\) be the region bounded above by the graph of \(y = e^x,\) below by the \(x\)-axis, left by the \(y\)-axis, and right by the line \(x = m.\) How fast is the area of \(R\) changing with \(m\) when \(m = 3 \ques\)
\(45.\)

\(46.\)
An object on the \(x\)-axis initially located at \(x = -2\) begins moving with velocity \(v(t) = 8t + 1,\) where \(t\) is time. What is the object's position when \(t = 3 \ques\)
\(47.\)
A car drives along the \(x\)-axis with an initial velocity of \(10 \undiv{m}{s}.\) It then accelerates as modeled by \(a(t) = 8 - 2t,\) where \(t\) is time measured in seconds. Determine the car's velocity after \(3\) seconds.
\(48.\)
A tank initially contains \(80\) liters of water. A hole is poked at the bottom of the tank, and water flows out at a rate of \(r(t) = (2 - t)^2\) liters per second. Find the volume in the tank after \(6\) seconds of leaking.
\(49.\)
In filtering a pool, water is pumped into the pool at a constant rate of \(4\) gallons per minute and pumped out at a rate of \(6t - 3t^2\) gallons per minute. Initially, the pool contains \(900\) gallons of water. Find the pool's volume after \(2\) minutes of filtering.
\(50.\)
Let \(g\) be a twice-differentiable function whose second derivative is continuous over \([1, 5].\) The slope of the tangent to \(g\) is positive at \(x = 1\) and negative at \(x = 5.\) Show that \(\int_1^5 g''(x) \di x \lt 0.\)
\(51.\)
Suppose \(g\) is a twice-differentiable function such that \(g''(x)\) is continuous over \(1 \leq x \leq 4.\) The line \(y - 3 = -5(x - 1)\) is tangent to the graph of \(y = g(x)\) at \(x = 1.\) Calculate \(g'(4)\) if \(\int_1^4 g''(x) \di x = 2.\)
\(52.\)
Let \(f\) be a positive, continuous function whose antiderivative is \(F.\) Consider the area of the region bounded above by the graph of \(y = f(x),\) below by the \(x\)-axis, left by the line \(x = a,\) and right by the line \(x = m.\) If \(m\) is doubled, then the area is doubled. Show that \(F(a) = 2F(m) - F(2m).\)
\(53.\)
The function \(v\) is continuous and has an antiderivative \(V.\) If \(\int_a^{2b} v(x) \di x = 0,\) then derive an expression for \(\int_a^b v(x) \di x\) in terms of \(V\) and \(b.\)
\(54.\)
Consider the region in the first quadrant bounded above by the hyperbola \(y = 1/x,\) below by the \(x\)-axis, left by the line \(x = m,\) and right by the line \(x = 2m.\) Prove that the area of the region is independent of \(m.\)
\(55.\)
Let \(S\) be the region in the first quadrant bounded by the graph of \(y = \sqrt x\) and the line \(x = m.\) The value of \(m\) is increasing at a constant rate of \(3\) units per minute. How quickly is the area of \(S\) increasing with time when \(m = 16 \ques\)
\(56.\)
Let \(Q\) be a quantity that changes with time \(t\) and whose value at \(t = 0\) is \(Q_0.\) Suppose that \(Q(t)\) increases at a rate of \(f(t)\) and decreases at a rate of \(g(t),\) where \(f\) and \(g\) are differentiable functions. Prove that \(Q\) has a relative maximum at the critical number \(t = c\) when \(f'(c) \lt g'(c).\)
