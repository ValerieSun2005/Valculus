\(26.\)

\(27.\)

\(28.\)
The Harmonic series diverges very slowly. An untrained student may fallaciously conclude that it converges after examining its partial sums.
  1. Interpreting the Harmonic series as a right-endpoint approximation with \(\Delta x = 1\) for the area under \(y = 1/x\) from \(x = 1\) to \(x = N,\) show that \[\sum_{i = 1}^N \frac{1}{i} \leq 1 + \ln N \pd\]
  2. Using the result in part (a), show that the sum of the first million terms of the Harmonic series is less than \(15\) and that the sum of its first billion terms is less than \(22.\)
  3. Using the result in part (a), estimate many terms in the Harmonic series are needed to attain a sum of \(50.\)
