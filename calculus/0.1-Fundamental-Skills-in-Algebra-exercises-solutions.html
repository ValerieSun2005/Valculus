EXERCISE 1
For
\[\ds x^5 + 4x^3 + 7x^5 - 1 + 8x^2 + 9x + 2\]
simplify the expression.
SOLUTION Let's add like terms: Adding \(x^5\) and \(7x^5\) gives \(8x^5,\) and adding \(-1\) and \(2\) gives \(1.\) So the expression is \[\boxed{8x^5 + 4x^3 + 8x^2 + 9x + 1}\]

For
\[\ds 8x + 2 = 18\]
solve the equation.
SOLUTION The objective is to isolate \(x.\) On both sides, subtracting \(2\) and then dividing by \(8\) give \[ \ba 8x &= 16 \nl \implies x &= \boxed 2 \ea \]
EXERCISE 7
For
\[\ds 6x + 4 - x = 8 + 2x + 5x\]
solve the equation.
SOLUTION The objective is to isolate \(x.\) Combining like terms on both sides, we get \[5x + 4 = 7x + 8 \pd\] On both sides, we subtract \(7x\) and subtract \(4;\) doing so yields \[-2x = 4 \pd\] Dividing both sides by \(-2\) shows \(\boxed{x = -2}.\)
EXERCISE 8

\[\ds 3(u + 5) - 2u = 7\]

SOLUTION Expanding the parentheses and combining like terms, we attain \[ \ba 3u + 15 - 2u &= 7 \nl u + 15 &= 7 \pd \ea \] Subtracting \(15\) from both sides gives \(\boxed{u = -8}.\)
EXERCISE 9
For
\[\ds 4(1 - 2x) + 2(x + 3) = 8 - x\]
solve the equation.
SOLUTION Expanding the parentheses and combining like terms, we attain \[ \ba 4 - 8x + 2x + 6 &= 8 - x \nl 10 - 6x &= 8 - x \pd \ea \] Adding \(6x\) and subtracting \(8\) on both sides yield \[2 = 5x \pd\] Dividing both sides by \(5\) shows \(\boxed{x = 2/5}.\)
EXERCISE 10
For
\[\ds \tfrac{1}{2} x + 3 = \tfrac{3}{2} + 5x\]
solve the equation.
SOLUTION It's easiest to first multiply both sides by \(2,\) thus clearing the denominators of \(1/2\) and \(3/2.\) Doing so gives \[x + 6 = 3 + 10x \pd\] To isolate \(x,\) we subtract \(x\) and subtract \(3\) from both sides, attaining \[3 = 9x \pd\] Then dividing both sides by \(9\) gives \(x = 3/9,\) or \(\boxed{x = 1/3}.\)
EXERCISE 11
For
\[\ds \tfrac{2}{3} t + 8 - 4t = \tfrac{3}{5} - t\]
solve the equation.
SOLUTION Let's begin by clearing the fractions. To eliminate the denominator of \(2/3,\) we multiply both sides by \(3 \col\) \[2t + 24 - 12t = \tfrac{9}{5} - 3t \pd\] To clear the denominator of \(9/5,\) we multiply both sides by \(5 \col\) \[10t + 120 - 60t = 9 - 15t \pd\] Combining like terms gives \[-50t + 120 = 9 - 15t \pd\] To isolate \(t,\) we add \(50 t\) and subtract \(9\) on both sides. Doing so yields \[111 = 35 t \pd\] Lastly, dividing both sides by \(35\) gives \(\boxed{t = 111/35}.\)
EXERCISE 12
For
\[\ds \tfrac{2}{3}(x + 4) + \tfrac{1}{6} (5x - 2) = x + 3\]
solve the equation.
SOLUTION It is easiest to first multiply both sides by \(6\) to clear the denominators of the fractions. Note that \(\tfrac{2}{3} \times \tfrac{6}{1}\) \(= 4.\) Doing so, expanding the parentheses, and combining like terms, we attain \[ \ba 4(x + 4) + (5x - 2) &= 6 (x + 3) \nl 4x + 16 + 5x - 2 &= 6x + 18 \nl 9x + 14 &= 6x + 18 \pd \ea \] On both sides we subtract \(6x\) and subtract \(14 \col\) \[3x = 4 \pd\] Dividing both sides by \(4\) gives \(\boxed{x = 4/3}.\)

EXERCISE 34
For
\[\ds y^3 - 8y^2 + 2y - 16\]
factor the expression completely.
SOLUTION No GCF exists, so we skip the preliminary step of extracting one. To factor by grouping, we split the expression into two pairs and factor each independently by extracting GCFs: \[ \underbrace{y^3 - 8y^2}_{\orange{y^2} (y - 8)} + \underbrace{2y + 16}_{\orange{2}(y - 8)} \pd \] Each pair shares the common factor \((y - 8),\) and the sum of the GCFs is \((\orange{y^2} + \orange{2}).\) The factored form is therefore \[\boxed{(y - 8)(\orange{y^2} + \orange{2})}\] Note that \((\orange{y^2} + \orange{2})\) is prime; it can't be factored any further.

EXERCISE 40
Prove the following statements with nested fractions, assuming that no division by \(0\) is present.
  1. \(\ds \frac{1}{1/a} = a\)
  2. \(\ds\frac{1}{a/b} = \frac{b}{a}\)
  3. \(\ds \frac{1/a}{b} = \frac{1}{ab}\)
SOLUTION Dividing two fractions is equivalent to multiplying the first fraction by the reciprocal of the second.
  1. Since \(1\) \(= 1/1,\) we have \[\frac{1}{1} \cdot \frac{a}{1} = \frac{a}{1} = a \cma\] as requested.
  2. The ratio is equivalent to \[1 \cdot \frac{b}{a} = \frac{1}{1} \cdot \frac{b}{a} = \frac{b}{a} \cma\] as requested.
  3. Since \(b\) \(= b/1,\) whose reciprocal is \(1/b,\) we have \[\frac{1}{a} \cdot \frac{1}{b} = \frac{1}{ab} \cma\] as requested.
EXERCISE 41
For
\[x^2 = 5x - 4\]
solve the equation.
SOLUTION Moving all terms to the left and factoring, we have \[ \ba x^2 - 5x + 4 &= 0 \nl (x - 4)(x - 1) &= 0 \pd \ea \] By the Zero Property of Multiplication, the left side equals \(0\) if at least one factor equals \(0.\) Equating each factor to \(0,\) we see \[ \baat{2} x - 4 &= 0 \qquad &x - 1 &= 0 \nl \implies x &= \boxed 4 &\qquad \implies x &= \boxed 1 \eaat \]
EXERCISE 42
For
\[x^2 = x\]
solve the equation.
SOLUTION It is inaccurate to divide both sides by \(x.\) Instead, let's write \[ \ba x^2 - x &= 0 \nl x(x - 1) &= 0 \pd \ea \] By the Zero Property of Multiplication, the left side equals \(0\) if at least one factor equals \(0.\) Equating each factor to \(0,\) we see \(\boxed{x = 0}\) and \(\boxed{x = 1}.\) If we divided both sides by \(x,\) then we would have missed the solution \(x = 0.\)

solve the equation.
