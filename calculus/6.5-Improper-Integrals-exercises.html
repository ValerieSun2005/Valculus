6.5 Exercises: Improper Integrals

Click on a problem number to see its solution.

For each of exercises 1–4, determine whether the integral is ordinary or improper. If it is improper, explain why.

\(\hspace{0.56em}1.\) \(\ds \int_1^\infty \frac{1}{x^3} \di x\) \(\hspace{0.56em}2.\) \(\ds \int_1^3 \frac{2}{x - 3} \di x\) \(\hspace{0.56em}3.\) \(\ds \int_0^4 \frac{1}{t - 5} \di t\)
\(\hspace{0.56em}4.\) \(\ds \int_0^2 \frac{1}{x^2 - 1} \di x\)

For each of exercises 5–18, evaluate the integral or justify that it diverges.

\(\hspace{0.56em}5.\) \(\ds \int_2^\infty \frac{1}{x + 9} \di x\) \(\hspace{0.56em}6.\) \(\ds \int_1^\infty \frac{3}{2\sqrt{x}} \di x\) \(\hspace{0.56em}7.\) \(\ds \int_3^\infty \frac{1}{x\sqrt[4]{x}} \di x\)
\(\hspace{0.56em}8.\) \(\ds \int_{-\infty}^\infty \frac{1}{x^2 + 1} \di x\) \(\hspace{0.56em}9.\) \(\ds \int_{-\infty}^{-1} \frac{1}{x^2} \di x\) \(10.\) \(\ds \int_0^\infty \sin x \di x\)
\(11.\) \(\ds \int_2^5 \frac{1}{x - 4} \di x\) \(12.\) \(\ds \int_0^\infty \frac{1}{(t + 2)^{3/2}} \di t\) \(13.\) \(\ds \int_0^1 \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}} \di x\)
\(14.\) \(\ds \int_{-2}^4 \frac{1}{(x - 1)^4} \di x\) \(15.\) \(\ds \int_{0}^{\pi/2} \sec \theta \tan \theta \di \theta\) \(16.\) \(\ds \int_4^\infty \frac{3}{x \ln x} \di x\)
\(17.\) \(\ds \int_0^1 t \ln t \di t\) \(18.\) \(\ds \int_0^\infty xe^{-x^2} \di x\)

For each of exercises 19–21, find the values of \(n\) such that the integral converges.

\(19.\) \(\ds \int_{1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{x^{1 - 3n}} \di x\) \(20.\) \(\ds \int_{2}^{\infty} \frac{1}{x^{5n - 1}} \di x\) \(21.\) \(\ds \int_{2}^{\infty} \frac{1}{x^{n^2 - 4n + 4}} \di x\)
\(22.\)
Let \(f\) be a continuous function such that \(0 \leq f(x) \leq 1/x^{4/3}.\) Show that \(\int_1^\infty f(x) \di x\) converges and is no greater than \(3.\)
\(23.\)
It is known that \(0 \leq 1/x^{5/9} \leq g(x),\) where \(g\) is a continuous function. Show that \(\int_1^\infty g(x) \di x\) is divergent.
\(24.\)
Let \(g\) be a differentiable function that satisfies \(g(0) = 0\) and \(g(4) = 12.\) Evaluate \(\int_0^4 [g'(x)/g(x)] \di x\) or show that it diverges.
\(25.\)
Let \(g\) and \(q\) be two continuous functions such that \(g(x) \leq q(x).\) Suppose \(\int_2^\infty q(x) \di x\) converges. Explain why \(\int_2^\infty g(x) \di x\) could be divergent.
\(26.\)
If \(f(x)\) is continuous, positive, and decreasing for all \(x \geq 1,\) and \(\lim_{x \to \infty} f(x) = 0,\) then must \(\int_1^\infty f(x) \di x\) be convergent?
\(27.\)
The rate at which people like a social media post is given by \(S(t) = 200e^{-t/10},\) where \(t\) is time in hours. How many likes will the post receive?
\(28.\)
For \(t \gt 1,\) a particle moves indefinitely along a line at a velocity given by \(v(t) = 2/t^{2/3},\) where \(v(t)\) is measured in meters per second and \(t\) is measured in seconds. Show that the particle travels an infinite distance.
\(29.\)
The density of trees in a forest surrounding a campsite is modeled by \[T(r) = \frac{290}{r^3 + 8r^2 + 16r} \comma \] where \(r\) is the distance, in meters, from the campsite and \(T(r)\) is measured in trees per square meter. The number of trees within \(K\) meters of the campsite is given by \(\int_0^K 2 \pi r \, T(r) \di r.\) Explain why the total number of trees in the forest is less than \(700.\)
