1.3 Exercise Solutions — Evaluating Limits Algebraically

EXERCISE 1
For \[\ds \lim_{t \to 9} \frac{t^2 - 81}{t - 9}\comma \] evaluate the limit if it exists.
SOLUTION The numerator is a difference of squares: \[t^2 - 81 = (t + 9)(t - 9) \period\] Thus, we rewrite the limit as \[ \ba \lim_{t \to 9} \frac{(t + 9)(t - 9)}{t - 9} &= \lim_{t \to 9} (t + 9) \nl &= \boxed{18} \ea \]
EXERCISE 2
For \[\ds \lim_{x \to 1} \frac{x^2 + 2x - 3}{x - 1}\comma \] evaluate the limit if it exists.
SOLUTION We factor the numerator as \[x^2 + 2x - 3 = (x + 3)(x - 1) \period\] The limit then becomes \[ \ba \lim_{x \to 1} \frac{(x + 3)(x - 1)}{x - 1} &= \lim_{x \to 1} (x + 3) \nl &= \boxed{4} \ea \]
EXERCISE 3
For \[\ds \lim_{x \to 3} \frac{x + 3}{x^2 - 9}\comma \] evaluate the limit if it exists.
SOLUTION The denominator is factored as \[x^2 - 9 = (x + 3)(x - 3) \period\] Therefore, we re-express the limit as \[\lim_{x \to 3} \frac{x + 3}{(x + 3)(x - 3)} = \lim_{x \to 3} \frac{1}{x - 3} \period\] The graph of \(y = \frac{1}{x - 3}\) has a vertical asymptote at \(x = 3.\) As \(x \to 3^-,\) \(\frac{1}{x - 3} \to - \infty.\) As \(x \to 3^+,\) \(\frac{1}{x - 3} \to +\infty.\) Because these one-sided limits aren't equivalent, \(\lim_{x \to 3} \frac{1}{x - 3}\) does not exist. and so \(\lim_{x \to 3} \frac{x + 3}{x^2 - 9}\) also does not exist.
EXERCISE 4
For \[\ds \lim_{\theta \to \pi/2} \frac{1 - \sin^2 \theta}{\cos \theta} \comma \] evaluate the limit if it exists.
SOLUTION From the Pythagorean identity, we get \[\sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta = 1 \implies 1 - \sin^2 \theta = \cos^2 \theta \period\] Thus, we re-express the numerator and the limit becomes \[ \ba \lim_{\theta \to \pi/2} \frac{\cos^2 \theta}{\cos \theta} &= \lim_{\theta \to \pi/2} \cos \theta \nl &= \cos \left(\frac{\pi}{2} \right) \nl &= \boxed{0} \ea \]
EXERCISE 5
For \[\ds \lim_{\theta \to 0} \frac{\sin 2 \theta}{\sin \theta}\comma \] evaluate the limit if it exists.
SOLUTION The double-angle identity of sine is \[\sin 2 \theta = 2 \sin \theta \cos \theta \period\] Therefore, the limit can be rewritten as \[ \ba \lim_{\theta \to 0} \frac{2 \sin \theta \cos \theta}{\sin \theta} &= \lim_{\theta \to 0} 2 \cos \theta \nl &= 2 \cos 0 \nl &= \boxed{2} \ea \]
EXERCISE 6
For \[\ds \lim_{\theta \to \pi/4} \frac{\cos 2 \theta}{\cos \theta - \sin \theta}\comma \] evaluate the limit if it exists.
SOLUTION Using the double-angle identity of cosine, we rewrite the numerator as \[ \ba \cos 2 \theta &= \cos^2 \theta - \sin^2 \theta \nl &= (\cos \theta + \sin \theta)(\cos \theta - \sin \theta) \period \ea \] The limit then becomes \[ \ba \lim_{\theta \to \pi/4} \frac{(\cos \theta + \sin \theta)(\cos \theta - \sin \theta)}{\cos \theta - \sin \theta} &= \lim_{\theta \to \pi/4} (\cos \theta + \sin \theta) \nl &= \cos \left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right) + \sin \left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right) \nl &= \boxed{\sqrt 2} \ea \]
EXERCISE 7
For \[\ds \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{\frac{1}{x} - \frac{1}{4}}{x - 4}\comma \] evaluate the limit if it exists.
SOLUTION Let's merge the fractions in the numerator of the limit. We obtain \[\frac{1}{x} - \frac{1}{4} = \frac{4 - x}{4x} \period\] As a result, our limit becomes \[ \ba \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{\frac{4 - x}{4x}}{x - 4} &= \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{-1}{4x} \nl &= \boxed{-\frac{1}{16}} \ea \]
EXERCISE 8
For \[\ds \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\frac{2}{x - 2} + 1}{x}\comma \] evaluate the limit if it exists.
SOLUTION Simplifying the expression \(\frac{2}{x - 2} + 1,\) we get \[\frac{2 + (x - 2)}{x - 2} = \frac{x}{x - 2} \period\] Therefore, we rewrite the limit as \[ \ba \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\frac{x}{x - 2}}{x} &= \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1}{x - 2} \nl &= \boxed{-\frac{1}{2}} \ea \]
EXERCISE 9
For \[\ds \lim_{t \to 1} \frac{t - 1}{1 - \sqrt t} \comma \] evaluate the limit if it exists.
SOLUTION The numerator is a difference of squares, \[t - 1 = (\sqrt{t} + 1)(\sqrt{t} - 1) \comma\] so we rewrite the limit as \[ \ba \lim_{t \to 1} \frac{(\sqrt{t} + 1)(\sqrt{t} - 1)}{1 - \sqrt t} &= \lim_{t \to 1} -(\sqrt t + 1) \nl &= \boxed{-2} \ea \]
EXERCISE 10
For \[\ds \lim_{x \to 7} \frac{7 - x}{2 - \sqrt{11 - x}}\comma \] evaluate the limit if it exists.
SOLUTION This limit is in the indeterminate form \(\frac{0}{0};\) yet we have no obvious simplifications, such as factoring or simplifying using trig identities. But we have a radical in the denominator, so let's try to eliminate it. We'll rationalize the denominator by multiplying the numerator and denominator each by the denominator's conjugate, \(2 + \sqrt{11 - x}.\) We then get \[ \ba \lim_{x \to 7} \frac{7 - x}{2 - \sqrt{11 - x}} \cdot \frac{2 + \sqrt{11 - x}}{2 + \sqrt{11 - x}} &= \lim_{x \to 7} \frac{(7 - x)(2 + \sqrt{11 - x})}{4 - (11 - x)} \nl &= \lim_{x \to 7} \frac{-(x - 7)(2 + \sqrt{11 - x})}{x - 7} \nl &= \lim_{x \to 7} -(2 + \sqrt{11 - x}) \nl &= \boxed{-4} \ea \] Because we rationalized the denomintor only, be sure not to expand the numerator: do not expand \((x - 7)(2 + \sqrt{11 - x}).\) The purpose of rationalization is to expose a common factor, \((x - 7)\) in this case.
EXERCISE 11
For \[\ds \lim_{u \to 3} \frac{\sqrt{u - 2} - 1}{2u - 6}\comma \] evaluate the limit if it exists.
SOLUTION We have no immediate simplifications for this limit. But the numerator has a radical, so we attempt to rationalize the numerator by multiplying the numerator and denominator by the numerator's conjugate, \(\sqrt{u - 2} + 1.\) We get \[ \ba \lim_{u \to 3} \frac{\sqrt{u - 2} - 1}{2u - 6} &= \lim_{u \to 3} \frac{\sqrt{u - 2} - 1}{2u - 6} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{u - 2} + 1}{\sqrt{u - 2} + 1} \nl &= \lim_{u \to 3} \frac{(u - 2) - 1}{(2u - 6)(\sqrt{u - 2} + 1)} \nl &= \lim_{u \to 3} \frac{u - 3}{2(u - 3)(\sqrt{u - 2} + 1)} \nl &= \lim_{u \to 3} \frac{1}{2(\sqrt{u - 2} + 1)} \nl &= \boxed{\frac{1}{4}} \ea \] This problem shows that rationalizing may yield a common factor to cancel—exactly what we want! When rationalizing the numerator, it's important to NOT expand the terms in the denominator; that is, we do not expand \((2u - 6)(\sqrt{u - 2} + 1).\)
EXERCISE 12
For \[\ds \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{\sqrt{13 - x} - 3}{\sqrt{x} - 2}\comma \] evaluate the limit if it exists.
SOLUTION This problem has radicals on the numerator and denominator. Let's rationalize the numerator by multiplying the numerator and denominator each by \(\sqrt{13 - x} + 3.\) Doing so produces \[ \ba \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{\sqrt{13 - x} - 3}{\sqrt x - 2} &= \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{\sqrt{13 - x} - 3}{\sqrt x - 2} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{13 - x} + 3}{\sqrt{13 - x} + 3} \nl &= \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{(13 - x) - 9}{(\sqrt x - 2)(\sqrt{13 - x} + 3)} \nl &= \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{4 - x}{(\sqrt x - 2)(\sqrt{13 - x} + 3)} \nl &= \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{(2 + \sqrt x)(2 - \sqrt x)}{-(2 - \sqrt x)(\sqrt{13 - x} + 3)} \nl &= \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{2 + \sqrt x}{-(\sqrt{13 - x} + 3)} \nl &= \boxed{-\frac{2}{3}} \ea \] Note that we also could have multiplied the numerator and denominator each by \(\sqrt x + 2,\) the conjugate of the denominator. But this process is longer: we would then have to multiply the numerator and denominator each by \(\sqrt{13 - x} + 3\) to expose a common factor, as you should verify.
EXERCISE 13
For \[\ds \lim_{x \to 10} \frac{\sqrt{26 - x} - 4}{\sqrt{19 - x} - 3}\comma \] evaluate the limit if it exists.
SOLUTION Let's start by rationalizing the numerator, although this choice won't matter. We multiply the numerator and denominator each by \(\sqrt{26 - x} + 4,\) yielding \[ \ba \lim_{x \to 10} \frac{\sqrt{26 - x} - 4}{\sqrt{19 - x} - 3} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{26 - x} + 4}{\sqrt{26 - x} + 4} &= \lim_{x \to 10} \frac{10 - x}{(\sqrt{19 - x} - 3)(\sqrt{26 - x} + 4)} \period \nl \ea \] Again, we don't have any resources. Now we multiply the numerator and denominator each by \(\sqrt{19 - x} + 3,\) giving \[ \ba \lim_{x \to 10} \frac{10 - x}{(\sqrt{19 - x} - 3)(\sqrt{26 - x} + 4)} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{19 - x} + 3}{\sqrt{19 - x} + 3} &= \lim_{x \to 10} \frac{(10 - x)(\sqrt{19 - x} + 3)}{(10 - x)(\sqrt{26 - x} + 4)} \nl &= \lim_{x \to 10} \frac{\sqrt{19 - x} + 3}{\sqrt{26 - x} + 4} \nl &= \boxed{\frac{3}{4}} \ea \] In general, if you have radicals on the numerator and denominator, then you should rationalize both. The order of rationalization does not matter.
