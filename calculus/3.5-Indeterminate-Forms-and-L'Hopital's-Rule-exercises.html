3.5 Exercises — Indeterminate Forms and L'Hopital's Rule

Click on a problem number to see its solution.

For each of exercises 1–22, evaluate the limit if it exists.

\(1.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{2x}{\sin x }\) \(2.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x - 2}{3\sin(x - 2)}\) \(3.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{\sqrt[3]{x + 3}}{\ln x}\)
\(4.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to 4} \frac{x - 4}{8x - 16 - x^2} \) \(5.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to \pi/2} \tan(x) \left(x - \frac{\pi}{2}\right)\) \(6.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to \infty} x^2e^{-x}\)
\(7.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0} x^3 \csc x\) \(8.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to -\infty} x^2e^{3x}\) \(9.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to 2^-} \tan(x - 2) \ln(2 - x) \)
\(10.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to \infty} [x^2 - e^x] \) \(11.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to \infty} \Big(\sqrt{x^2 + 4x} - x \Big) \) \(12.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to 1^+} \left(\frac{1}{x - 1} - \frac{1}{\ln x} \right)\)
\(13.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0} \left(\csc x - \frac{2}{x}\right)\) \(14.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to 1^+} \left(\frac{1}{\ln x} - \frac{2}{\sqrt{x - 1}} \right)\) \(15.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to 1^+} \left(\frac{2}{3x - 3} \right)^{\ln x }\)
\(16.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to 1^+} \left(1 + \ln x \right)^{2/(x - 1)}\) \(17.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0^-} \left(1 + \sin x\right)^{1/x^2}\) \(18.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0^+} \left(\tan x \right)^x\)
\(19.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to \infty} \left(\sqrt{x}\right)^{e^{-x}}\) \(20.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0^+} \left(\csc x \right)^{x}\) \(21.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0^+} \left(x\right)^{1/\ln x}\)
\(22.\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0^+} \left(1 + \frac{2}{x}\right)^{1/(x \ln x)}\)
\(23.\)
Let \(g\) be a twice-differentiable function. It is known that \(g(4) = 2\) and \(g'(4) = 6.\) Find \[\lim_{x \to 4} \frac{2 - g(x)}{\sin(x - 4)}\] if it exists.
\(24.\)
Let \(f\) and \(g\) be twice-differentiable functions. It is known that \(\ds \lim_{x \to \infty} f(x) = 0,\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to \infty} g(x) = 0,\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to \infty} f'(x) = 5,\) and \(\ds \lim_{x \to \infty} g'(x) = 8.\) Find \[\lim_{x \to \infty} \left(\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\right)^2\] if it exists.
\(25.\)
Let \(f\) be a twice-differentiable function. It is known that \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0} f(x) = 3,\) \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0} f'(x) = 0,\) and \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0} f''(x) = 1.\) Find \[\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{4f(x) - 12 \cos x}{x^2}\] if it exists.
\(26.\)
Let \(p\) be a twice-differentiable function. Let \(q\) be a twice-differentiable function defined by \[q(x) = \frac{2[p(x)]^3 + 4}{x^2 - 6x}\] that satisfies \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0} q(x) = 12.\) It is known that \(\ds \lim_{x \to 0} q(x)\) can be evaluated using L'Hôpital's Rule. Find \(p(0)\) and \(p'(0).\)
\(27.\)
Consider the family of functions \(k(x) = \ln(x)/x^p.\) Evaluate \(\ds \lim_{x \to \infty} k(x)\) for
  1. \(p \gt 0\)
  2. \(p \lt 0.\)
\(28.\)
Determine whether \(f(x) = 2 \sqrt[3]{x}\) or \(g(x) = \ln(x - 4)\) grows fastest.
\(29.\)
Determine whether \(f(x) = 2x^6 - 4x^5 + 2\) or \(g(x) = e^x\) grows fastest.
\(30.\)
Show that \(x^n\) grows faster than does \(\ln x\) for all positive \(n.\)
