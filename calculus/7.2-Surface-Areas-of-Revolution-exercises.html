\(16.\)
By revolving the semicircle \(y = \sqrt{r^2 - x^2}\) about the \(x\)-axis, prove that a sphere of radius \(r\) has a surface area of \(S = 4 \pi r^2.\)
\(17.\)
Let \(f\) be a positive, smooth function over \([a, b].\) Region \(R\) is the region bounded by the graph of \(y = f(x),\) the \(x\)-axis, and the lines \(x = a\) and \(x = b.\) Solid \(S\) is the solid generated by revolving \(R\) around the \(x\)-axis. Prove that the surface area of \(S\) is at least \(2 \pi\) times greater than the area of \(R.\)
\(18.\)
A solid is generated by rotating the region bounded by the infinite curve \(y = e^{-x}\) in the first quadrant about the \(x\)-axis. Calculate the surface area of this solid.
\(19.\)
Gabriel's Horn is the solid generated by rotating the region bounded by \(y = 1/x,\) the \(x\)-axis, and the line \(x = 1\) about the \(x\)-axis (Figure 7). Show that the solid has infinite surface area but finite volume.
