9.2 Exercises — Differentiating Polar Functions

Click on a problem number to see its solution.

For each of exercises 1–8, calculate and interpret the derivative.

\(1.\) \(\ds r = 2 + \cos \theta \cma \deriv{r}{\theta} \intEval_{\theta = \pi/2}\) \(2.\) \(\ds r = 4\theta + \sin \theta \cma \deriv{r}{\theta} \intEval_{\theta = \pi}\)
\(3.\) \(\ds r = \sin \theta \cma \deriv{y}{\theta} \intEval_{\theta = -\pi/6}\) \(4.\) \(\ds r = 3 \sin 2 \theta \cma \deriv{r}{\theta} \intEval_{\theta = \pi/3}\)
\(5.\) \(\ds r = 4 \sin \theta \cma \deriv{x}{\theta} \intEval_{\theta = 7\pi/6}\) \(6.\) \(\ds r = \sec \theta \cma \deriv{x}{\theta} \intEval_{\theta = 3 \pi/4}\)
\(7.\) \(\ds r = \cos 5 \theta \cma \deriv{y}{\theta} \intEval_{\theta = \pi/2}\) \(8.\) \(\ds r = \theta \cma \deriv{y}{\theta} \intEval_{\theta = 3\pi/2}\)

For each of exercises 9–16, calculate the slope of the line tangent to the polar graph at the given angle.

\(9.\) \(\ds r = 2 + \cos \theta \cma \theta = \frac{\pi}{4}\) \(10.\) \(\ds r = \cos 3 \theta \cma \theta = 0\)
\(11.\) \(\ds r = \theta^2 \cma \theta = \pi\) \(12.\) \(\ds r = 2 \sin \theta \cma \theta = \frac{3 \pi}{2}\)
\(13.\) \(\ds r = \cos 3 \theta \cma \theta = \frac{\pi}{3}\) \(14.\) \(\ds r = 1 - 2 \sin \theta \cma \theta = \frac{\pi}{2}\)
\(15.\) \(\ds r = \sqrt \theta \cma \theta = \pi\) \(16.\) \(\ds r = \sin 2 \theta \cma \theta = \frac{\pi}{2}\)
\(17.\)
Determine an equation of the line tangent to the graph of \(r = 2\) when \(\theta = -\pi/4.\)
\(18.\)
Find an equation of the line tangent to the graph of \(r = 4 \cos \theta\) when \(\theta = 5 \pi/6.\)
\(19.\)
The polar graph of \(r = k\) is a circle of radius \(\abs k\) centered at the origin. Prove that the slope of the line tangent to the circle at any angle \(\theta\) is \(-\cot \theta.\)
\(20.\)
Consider the circle \(r = k \sin \theta.\) Prove that the slope of the line tangent to the circle at any angle \(\theta\) is \(\tan 2\theta.\)
\(21.\)
Prove that the slope of the line tangent to the circle \(r = k \cos \theta\) at any angle \(\theta\) is \(-\cot 2\theta.\)
\(22.\)
A particle travels along the polar graph of \(r = 1 + 3 \sin \theta\) with time \(t\) such that \(\textderiv{\theta}{t} = 2\) radians per second. Find and interpret \(\textderiv{r}{t}|_{\theta = \pi/3}.\)
\(23.\)
The polar graph of \(r = \sin 3 \theta\) is a rose of three petals. Find the vertex of the petal in the first quadrant.
\(24.\)
Identify the point on the polar graph \(r = 2 + \sin \theta\) that is farthest from the origin.
\(25.\)
The shape of a horse racetrack is given by the polar graph \(r = 2 + 2 \cos \theta,\) where \(r\) is measured in meters. An bell tower is located at the pole. A horse runs counterclockwise along the track at a constant angular speed of \(8\) radians per minute. When \(\theta = 3 \pi/2,\) is the horse moving toward or away from the bell tower?
\(26.\)
The family of polar functions \(r = a + b \sin \theta\) produces a limacon for nonzero \(a\) and \(b\) such that \(a \ne b.\) Show that the limacon has a horizontal tangent when \(\theta = \pi/2.\)
\(27.\)
For nonzero \(a,\) show that the family of cardioids \(r = a + a \cos \theta\) has a vertical tangent when \(\theta = 0.\)
