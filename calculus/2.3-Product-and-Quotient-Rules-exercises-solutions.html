EXERCISE 1
For \[\ds x \sin x\comma \] find the derivative.
SOLUTION The expression \(x \sin x\) is a product of two functions, \[f(x) = x \qquad g(x) = \sin x \pd\] Note that \(f'(x) = 1\) and \(g'(x) = \cos x.\) Thus, the Product Rule gives the derivative of \(x \sin x \) to be \[f'g + fg' = \boxed{\sin x + x \cos x}\]
EXERCISE 2
For \[\ds x^2 \cos x\comma \] find the derivative.
SOLUTION The expression \(x^2 \cos x\) is a product of \[f(x) = x^2 \qquad g(x) = \cos x \pd\] Differentiating \(f\) and \(g\) give \[f'(x) = 2x \qquad g'(x) = -\sin x \pd\] Then the Product Rule gives the derivative of \(x^2 \cos x\) to be \[ f'g + fg' = \boxed{2x \cos x - x^2 \sin x} \]
EXERCISE 3
For \[\ds (\sin x) \sqrt{x} \comma \] find the derivative.
SOLUTION We have a product with \[f(x) = \sin x \qquad g(x) = \sqrt{x} = x^{1/2} \pd\] Then \[f'(x) = \cos x \qquad g'(x) = \tfrac{1}{2} x^{-1/2} \pd\] Thus, the derivative of \((\sin x) \sqrt{x}\) is \[ \ba f'g + fg' &= \sqrt{x} \cos x + \tfrac{1}{2} x^{-1/2} \sin x \nl &= \boxed{\sqrt{x} \cos x + \frac{1}{2 \sqrt{x}} \sin x} \ea \]
EXERCISE 4
For \[\ds x \ln x\comma \] find the derivative.
SOLUTION In this product, we have \[ \baat{2} f(x) &= x \lspace g(x) &&= \ln x \nl f'(x) &= 1 \lspace g'(x) &&= \tfrac{1}{x} \pd \eaat \] The derivative of \(x \ln x\) is then \[f'g + fg' = \boxed{\ln x + 1}\]
EXERCISE 5
For \[\ds e^x \sin x\comma \] find the derivative.
SOLUTION The derivative of \(e^x\) is \(e^x,\) and the derivative of \(\sin x\) is \(\cos x.\) Therefore, the Product Rule shows the derivative of \(e^x \sin x\) to be \[\boxed{e^x \sin x + e^x \cos x}\]
EXERCISE 6
For \[\ds \frac{1}{x}\tan x\comma \] find the derivative.
SOLUTION We rewrite the expression as \(x^{-1} \tan x.\) We may use the Product Rule by writing \[ \baat{2} f(x) &= x^{-1} \quad \lspace g(x) &&= \tan x \nl f'(x) &= -x^{-2} \lspace g'(x) &&= \sec^2 x \pd \eaat \] Thus, the derivative is \[ \ba f'g + fg' &= -x^{-2} \tan x + x^{-1} \sec^2 x \nl &= \boxed{-\tfrac{1}{x^2} \tan x + \tfrac{1}{x} \sec^2 x} \ea \] It is also valid to use the Quotient Rule.
EXERCISE 7
For \[\ds \frac{\sin x}{x + 4}\comma \] find the derivative.
SOLUTION The expression is a quotient, \(f/g,\) of two functions: \[f(x) = \sin x \lspace g(x) = x + 4 \pd\] We note that \(f'(x) = \cos x\) and \(g'(x) = 1.\) By the Quotient Rule, the derivative of \(\sin x/(x + 4)\) is \[ \ba \frac{f'g - fg'}{g^2} &= \frac{(\cos x)(x + 4) - (\sin x)(1)}{x^2} \nl &= \boxed{\frac{(x + 4) \cos x - \sin x}{(x + 4)^2}} \ea \]
EXERCISE 8
For \[\ds \frac{\cos x}{x^2 + 4}\comma \] find the derivative.
SOLUTION We have a quotient \(f/g\) with \[f(x) = \cos x \lspace g(x) = x^2 + 4 \pd\] The derivatives of these functions are \(f'(x) = -\sin x\) and \(g'(x) = 2x.\) Thus, the derivative of \(\cos x/(x^2 + 4)\) is \[ \ba \frac{f'g - fg'}{g^2} &= \frac{(- \sin x)(x^2 + 4) - (\cos x)(2x)}{(x^2 + 4)^2} \nl &= \boxed{\frac{-(x^2 + 4) \sin x - 2x \cos x}{(x^2 + 4)^2}} \ea \]
EXERCISE 9
For \[\ds \frac{x^2}{\tan x}\comma \] find the derivative.
SOLUTION Using the Quotient Rule with \[ \baat{2} f(x) &= x^2 \lspace g(x) &&= \tan x \nl f'(x) &= 2x \lspace g'(x) &&= \sec^2 x \cma \eaat \] we obtain \[ \ba \frac{f'g - fg'}{g^2} &= \boxed{\frac{2x \tan x - x^2 \sec^2 x}{\tan^2 x}} \ea \]
EXERCISE 10
For \[\ds \frac{\cos x}{2x - 6}\comma \] find the derivative.
SOLUTION The derivative of the numerator is \((- \sin x),\) and the derivative of the denominator is \(2.\) Thus, the Quotient Rule gives the derivative of \(\cos x/(2x - 6)\) to be \[ \ba \frac{(-\sin x)(2x - 6) - (\cos x)(2)}{(2x - 6)^2} = \boxed{\frac{(6 - 2x) \sin x - 2 \cos x}{(2x - 6)^2}} \ea \]
EXERCISE 11
For \[\ds \frac{\cot x}{5\sqrt{x} - 3}\comma \] find the derivative.
SOLUTION The derivative of the numerator is \((-\csc^2 x),\) and the derivative of the denominator is \(5/(2 \sqrt x).\) Thus, the Quotient Rule gives the derivative of \(\cot x/(5\sqrt{x} - 3)\) to be \[ \ba \frac{(-\csc^2 x)(5\sqrt{x} - 3) - (\cot x) \par{\frac{5}{2 \sqrt x}} }{(5\sqrt{x} - 3)^2} = \boxed{\frac{(3 - 5 \sqrt{x}) \csc^2 x - \frac{5 \cot x}{2 \sqrt x}}{(5\sqrt{x} - 3)^2}} \ea \]
EXERCISE 12
For \[\ds x^2 e^x \cos x\comma \] find the derivative.
SOLUTION We have a product of three terms, but we can apply the Product Rule by grouping two of the factors. For example, let \[f(x) = x^2 e^x \qquad g(x) = \cos x \pd\] Then \[f'(x) = 2x e^x + x^2 e^x \qquad g'(x) = -\sin x \pd\] The derivative of \(x^2 e^x \cos x\) is then \[ \ba f'(x)g(x) + f(x)g'(x) &= (2x e^x + x^2 e^x) \cos x + (x^2 e^x) (-\sin x) \nl &= \boxed{2xe^x + x^2 e^x \cos x - x^2 e^x \sin x} \ea \]
EXERCISE 13
Let \(f\) be a differentiable function that satisfies \(f(3) = 2.\) The line \(y = 2 + \frac{1}{2}(x - 3)\) is tangent to the graph of \(y = f(x)\) at \(x = 3.\) Let \(k(x) = 2x^2 f(x).\) Find \(k'(3).\)
SOLUTION We differentiate \(k\) using the Product Rule to get \[ \ba k'(x) &= 4x f(x) + 2x^2 f'(x) \nl k'(3) &= 12 f(3) + 18 f'(3) \pd \ea \] We are given \(f(3) = 2.\) The tangent line to the graph of \(y = f(x)\) at \(x = 3\) has slope \(1/2,\) so \(f'(3) = 1/2.\) Plugging in these values gives \[k'(3) = 12(2) + 18 \par{\tfrac{1}{2}} = \boxed{33} \]
EXERCISE 14
Let \(f\) and \(g\) be differentiable functions such that \(f(2) = 2,\) \(f'(2) = -1,\) \(g(2) = 3,\) and \(g'(2) = -4.\) Find \(k'(2).\)
  1. \(\ds k(x) = f(x) g(x)\)
  2. \(\ds k(x) = \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\)
  3. \(\ds k(x) = \frac{g(x)}{f(x)}\)
  4. \(\ds k(x) = \frac{f(x)}{g(x) + f(x)}\)
SOLUTION
  1. The Product Rule gives \[k'(x) = f'(x) g(x) + f(x) g'(x) \pd\] Plugging in \(x = 2,\) we obtain \[ \ba k'(2) &= f'(2) g(2) + f(2) g'(2) \nl &= (-1)(3) + (2)(-4) \nl &= \boxed{-11} \ea \]
  2. Using the Quotient Rule, we obtain \[k'(x) = \frac{f'(x) g(x) - f(x) g'(x)}{[g(x)]^2}\] When \(x = 2,\) we have \[ \ba k'(2) &= \frac{f'(2) g(2) - f(2) g'(2)}{[g(2)]^2} \nl &= \frac{(-1)(3) - (2)(-4)}{3^2} \nl &= \boxed{\frac{5}{9}} \ea \]
  3. The Quotient Rule gives \[ \ba k'(x) &= \frac{g'(x) f(x) - g(x) f'(x)}{[f(x)]^2} \nl k'(2) &= \frac{g'(2) f(2) - g(2) f'(2)}{[f(2)]^2} \nl &= \frac{(-4)(2) - (3)(-1)}{2^2} \nl &= \boxed{-\frac{5}{4}} \ea \]
  4. Let us use \(f\) and \(g\) as abbreviations of \(f(x)\) and \(g(x),\) respectively. We differentiate \(k,\) getting \[ \ba k'(x) &= \frac{f'(g + f) - f(g' + f')}{(g + f)^2} \scol \nl k'(2) &= \frac{(-1)(3 + 2) - (2)(-4 - 1)}{(2 + 3)^2} \nl &= \boxed{\frac{1}{5}} \ea \]
