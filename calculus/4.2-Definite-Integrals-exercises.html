\(\hspace{0.56em}6.\)
Walkthrough of Evaluating Definite Integrals Let's compute the definite integral \(\int_0^4 2x \di x\) by evaluating the limit of a right Riemann sum of equal widths.
  1. For a right Riemann sum whose rectangles have equal widths, we use the formula \[\int_a^b f(x) \di x = \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{i = 1}^n f(x_i) \Delta x \cma\] where \(x_i = a + i \Delta x\) and \(\Delta x = (b - a)/n.\) Comparing this form to \(\int_0^4 2x \di x\) shows \(a = 0,\) \(b = 4,\) and \(f(x) = 2x.\) Find expressions for \(x_i\) and \(\Delta x.\)
  2. In terms of \(i\) and \(n,\) determine an expression for \(f(x_i) \Delta x.\)
  3. Simplify the limit in part (a) to show that \[\int_0^4 2x \di x = \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{32}{n^2} \sum_{i = 1}^n i \pd\]
  4. Use the formula \[\sum_{i = 1}^n i = \frac{n(n + 1)}{2}\] to convert the sum in part (c) to an algebraic expression. Evaluate the corresponding limit.
