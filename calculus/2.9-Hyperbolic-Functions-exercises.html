\(49.\)
It is often easiest to simplify a function before proceeding to differentiation.
  1. Prove that \[\frac{1 + \tanh x}{1 - \tanh x} = e^{2x} \pd\]
  2. Let \(k\) be a positive constant. Using the result in part (a), show that \[\deriv{}{x} \par{\frac{1 + \tanh x}{1 - \tanh x}}^k = 2 k x e^{2kx} \pd\]
  3. By differentiating the result in part (b), show that \[\derivOrder{}{x}{2} \par{\frac{1 + \tanh x}{1 - \tanh x}}^k = 4k^2 e^{2kx} \pd\]
  4. If \(n\) is any positive integer, then derive an expression for \[\derivOrder{}{x}{n} \par{\frac{1 + \tanh x}{1 - \tanh x}}^k \pd \]
