\(12.\)
In a competitive market, the price of a good is driven to the point at which quantity supplied equals quantity demanded. (Economists often call this phenomenon the invisible hand.) The intersection of the demand and supply curves is called the equilibrium point. Consider the small, competitive market for Product A, in which a supply function is \(S(x) = x^2 + 12\) and a demand function is \(D(x) = 18 - x.\)
  1. How many units of Product A are produced? (We call this number the equilibrium quantity.)
  2. Calculate the consumer surplus \(s_C.\)
  3. Calculate the producer surplus \(s_P.\)
\(13.\)
Total surplus is the sum of consumer surplus and producer surplus. We use total surplus to measure how beneficial a market is to a society, since high total surplus indicates a high level of satisfaction with a market. For a competitive market, the demand function is \(D(x) = 600 e^{-0.4 x}\) and the supply function is \(S(x) = x^2 + 40.\) Using a calculator, calculate the total surplus \(s_T\) to three decimal places.
