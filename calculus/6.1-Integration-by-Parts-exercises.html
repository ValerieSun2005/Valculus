\(24.\)
The function \(g\) has derivatives of all orders. It is known that \(g(1) = -3,\) \(g(4) = 7,\) and \(\int_1^4 g(x) \di x = 8.\) Calculate \(\int_1^4 x g'(x) \di x.\)
\(25.\)
Let \(f\) and \(g\) be twice-differentiable functions such that \(f(2) = 0,\) \(f(3) = -2,\) \(g(3) = 5,\) and \(\int_2^3 f(x) g'(x) \di x = 13.\) Evaluate \(\int_2^3 f'(x) g(x) \di x.\)
\(26.\)
Let \(p\) be a differentiable function. Show that \[\int_0^5 p(x) \di x = 5p(5) - \int_0^5 x p'(x) \di x \pd\]
\(27.\)
Prove that \[\int \frac{\sin x}{x} \di x = (\sin x) \ln \abs x - \int (\cos x) \ln \abs x \di x \pd\]
\(28.\)
Evaluate \(\int \ln x \di x\) by substituting \(w = \ln x\) and using the result of Example 1.

\(35.\)
Let region \(R\) be bounded above by the line \(y = \pi/4,\) below by the graph of \(y = \atan 2x,\) and on the left by the line \(x = -1/2.\) Calculate the area of \(R.\)
\(36.\)
The density of sprinkles on a circular cake as a function of the distance, \(r,\) from the center of the cake is given by \(s(r) = \tfrac{1}{10} e^{r/3},\) where \(r\) is measured in inches and \(s(r)\) is measured in sprinkles per square inch. The total number of sprinkles on the cake is given by \[T = 2 \pi \int_0^{10} r s(r) \di r \pd\] Calculate this number.
\(37.\)
A social media account gains followers according to the rate function \(f(t) = 10 - te^{-t/40},\) where \(t\) is time in hours. If the account initially has \(30\) followers, then calculate how many followers the user will have after \(6\) hours.
\(38.\)
The velocity of a car driving on a straight road as a function of time \(t\) is given by \(v(t) = t^2 e^{-t/6},\) where \(t\) is measured in seconds and \(v(t)\) is measured in meters per second. How far does the car travel in the first \(T\) seconds?
\(39.\)
Function \(k\) has a positive second derivative on \((0, 2).\) If \(k(0) = 1,\) \(k(2) = 3,\) and \(k'(2) = 4,\) then show that \[0 \leq \int_0^2 x k''(x) \sin x \di x \leq 6 \pd \]
\(40.\)
Solve the initial-value problem \[\deriv{y}{x} = x^3 \ln 7x \cmaa y(1) = 2 \pd\]
\(41.\)
Find the particular solution to the differential equation \(f'(x) = 2x \cos 4x\) such that \(f(0) = 3.\)
\(42.\)
The force exerted on a particle as it travels along the \(x\)-axis is given by \[F(x) = x e^{-6x} \cma\] where \(F(x)\) is measured in kilonewtons and \(x\) is measured in meters. How much work is done on the particle as it moves from \(x = 1\) to \(x = 2 \ques\)
\(43.\)
Determine the average value of \(f(x) = x e^{-x}\) over \(0 \leq x \leq 2.\)
\(44.\)
Calculate the average value of \(g(t) = t \sec^2 t\) over \(0 \leq t \leq \pi/4.\)
\(45.\)
The region bounded above by the graph of \(y = \cos x\) and the \(x\)-axis from \(x = 0\) to \(x = \pi/2\) is the base of a solid whose cross sections perpendicular to the \(x\)-axis are rectangles of height \(3x.\) Calculate the solid's volume.
\(46.\)
A solid's base is the region bounded by the \(y\)-axis, the curve \(y = e^{2x},\) and the line \(y = e^2.\) If cross sections perpendicular to the \(y\)-axis are rectangles of height \(y^2,\) then calculate the solid's volume.
\(47.\)
For positive \(n,\) prove the reduction formula \[\int \cos^n \theta \di \theta = \frac{1}{n} \sin \theta \cos^{n - 1} \theta + \frac{n - 1}{n} \int \cos^{n - 2} \theta \di \theta \pd \]
