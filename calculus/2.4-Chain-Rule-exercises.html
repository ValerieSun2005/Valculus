\(53.\)
A block of mass \(m\) is attached to a spring, whose spring constant (a measure of stiffness) is measured by a number \(k.\) Suppose that the block is stretched a distance \(A\) away from its resting position, the equilibrium position. The spring then exerts a restoring force that pushes the block back toward equilibrium; then, the block compresses the spring by the same amplitude \(A\) (Figure 1). The block's position from equilibrium then oscillates as the spring repeats this periodic motion. The displacement, \(x,\) of the block from equilibrium as a function of time \(t\) is given by \[x = A \cos \par{t \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}} \pd \] Show that the block's acceleration is given by \[a = -\frac{k}{m} x \pd\] This equation asserts that acceleration, caused by a restoring spring force, varies directly with the distance between the block and its equilibrium position. This relationship is called Hooke's Law.
