10.6 Exercises — Power Series

Click on a problem number to see its solution.

\(1.\)
What is a power series? What is the general form of a power series?
\(2.\)
Define the interval of convergence and radius of convergence.
\(3.\)
Suppose that function \(f(x)\) has a power series whose radius of convergence is \(R.\) What are the radii of convergence for the power series of \(f'(x)\) and \(\int f(x) \di x \ques\)
\(4.\)
The power series \(\sum_{n = 0}^\infty c_n (x - 2)^n\) has a radius of convergence of \(3.\) The series converges at \(x = -1\) and diverges at \(x = 5.\)
  1. Identify the center of the series.
  2. Determine the series' interval of convergence.

\(38.\)
Function \(f\) has the power series representation \(\sum_{n = 1}^\infty x^{2n}/n.\) Let \(g(x) = \int f(x) \di x\) and \(g(0) = 1.\) Determine a power series representation for \(g.\)
\(39.\)
Let \(f\) be a function that has the power series representation \[f(x) = \sum_{n = 0}^\infty (-1)^n \frac{x^{2n + 1}}{n + 2} \pd\] Function \(g\) is defined by \(g(x) = \int f(x) \di x\) and satisfies \(g(0) = 2.\) Find a power series representation for \(g.\)
\(40.\)
Function \(f\) has the power series representation \[f(x) = \sum_{n = 0}^\infty (-1)^n \frac{(x - 3)^{n + 4}}{(n + 1)!} \pd\] Suppose \(F\) is an antiderivative of \(f\) that satisfies \(F(3) = 4.\) Find a power series representation of \(F.\)
\(41.\)
Using the relationship \[\deriv{}{x} \parbr{\tan^{-1} x} = \frac{1}{1 + x^2} \cma\] find a power series for \(\tan^{-1} x.\) Then give the interval of convergence.
\(42.\)
Suppose that power series \(\sum_{n = 0}^\infty c_n x^n\) has a radius of convergence of \(R.\) What is the radius of convergence of \(\sum_{n = 0}^\infty c_n (2x)^n \ques\)
\(43.\)
We obtain power series for many functions by differentiating other, known power series.
  1. Show that \[\frac{1}{1 + x} = \sum_{n = 0}^\infty (-1)^n \, x^n \cma \abs x \lt 1 \pd\]
  2. Find a power series for \(1/(1 + x)^2.\)
  3. Using the result from part (b), determine a power series for \(1/(1 + x)^3.\)
\(44.\)
For all \(x,\) \[f(x) = \sum_{n = 0}^\infty \frac{x^n}{n!} \pd\]
  1. Show that \(f\) satisfies the differential equation \(f'(x) = f(x).\)
  2. Show that \(f(x) = e^x.\)
  3. Determine a power series for \(e^{2x}.\)
