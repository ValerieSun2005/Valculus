\(39.\)
A solution's \(\pH\) is defined by the logarithmic relationship \[\pH = -\log \, [\ce{H+}] \cma\] where \([\ce{H+}]\) is the concentration of hydrogen ions in moles per liter. Solutions with \(\pH\) values less than \(7\) are acidic and \(\pH\) values greater than \(7\) are basic. Calculate the \(\pH\) of each solution, and classify it as basic or acidic:
  1. a \(2\)-liter solution containing \(0.004\) mole of hydrogen ions
  2. a \(16\)-liter solution containing \(1.5 \times 10^{-8}\) mole of hydrogen ions
\(40.\)
Calculate the concentration of hydrogen ions, in moles per liter, of a solution whose \(\pH\) is \(6.\)
\(41.\)
The population of a small town grows exponentially according to the function \[P(t) = 9000(1.04)^t \cma\] where \(t\) is the number of years after 2020.
  1. Determine the population in 2020.
  2. In what year will the population reach \(11000 \ques\)
  3. After how many years does the population double? (This number is called the doubling time.)
\(42.\)
An earthquake's intensity can be measured by the Richter scale, a logarithmic scale based on the amplitudes of seismic waves—mechanical waves of acoustic energy formed by the sudden movement of materials within Earth. On March 11, 2011, a devastating earthquake of magnitude \(9.0\) struck Japan's northeastern region, right off the coast of Honshu, killing thousands. On August 24, 2014, an earthquake of magnitude \(6.0\) occurred in the San Francisco Bay Area. (Luckily, only one person died from the San Francisco earthquake.) Based on the seismic waves, how many times more intense was the earthquake in Japan than the earthquake in San Francisco?
\(43.\)

The coefficient of friction between the belt and shaft is \(\mu;\) large values of \(\mu\) indicate high levels of friction.
