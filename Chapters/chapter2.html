Chapter 2: Differentiation Rules

2.1     Defining a Derivative

Limit definition of a derivative, with geometric intuition and motivation. Finding tangent and normal lines to establish linear approximations. First- and second-order derivatives.

2.2     Differentiating Power, Exponential, and Sinusoidal Functions

Derivatives of exponential, polynomial, and sinusoidal functions. Discussion of tangent and normal lines.

2.3     Product Rule and Quotient Rule

Differentiating products and quotients of functions. Algebraic and graphical applications. Contains geometric intuition and proof. Derivatives of all trigonometric functions.

2.4     Chain Rule

Differentiating compositions of functions. Algebraic and graphical applications. Contains geometric intuition and proof.

2.5     Implicit Differentiation and Differentiating Inverse Functions

Differentiating implicit equations. First- and second-order implicit derivatives. Differentiating inverse trigonometric functions and logarithms (natural logarithms and other bases).

2.6     Logarithmic Differentiation

The use of natural logarithms to simplify products, quotients, and compositions of functions for easier differentiation.

2.7     Linearization and Differentials

Using linearization (tangent line approximations) to estimate values of functions. Geometric interpretations of differentials. Discussion of error and percent error.
