Chapter 3: Applications of Differentiation

In Chapter 2 we trained ourselves in differentiating various combinations of functions. We applied derivatives to calculating slopes of tangents and relating position, velocity, and acceleration. Yet there are countless more applications of differentiation, all under the same universal ideaâ€”rates of change. In this chapter we use derivatives to find a graph's peaks and troughs (3.1); prove the existence of a particular rate (3.2); describe a graph's shape (3.3); evaluate limits in indeterminate forms (3.4); optimize real-world quantities (3.5); analyze a firm's revenue, costs, and profits (3.6); and approximate the zeros of functions (3.7).

Sections

3.1     Minimum and Maximum Values

Overview of critical points and the First Derivative Test. Extreme Value Theorem to establish presence of a function's minimum or maximum. Process of finding relative and absolute extrema.

3.2     Mean Value Theorem and Rolle's Theorem

Overview and proof of Mean Value Theorem and Rolle's Theorem. Use of Rolle's Theorem to prove the existence of zeros on an interval.

3.3     Using the First and Second Derivatives

Discussion of first and second derivatives of a function and their effects on a graph. Concavity, second-derivative test, and inflection points.

3.4     Indeterminate Forms and L'Hopital's Rule

Evaluating limits of indeterminate forms: \(\indZero,\) \(\indInfty,\) \(0 \times \infty,\) \(\infty - \infty,\) \(0^0,\) \(1^\infty,\) and \(\infty^0.\) Discussion of relative growth and decay rates.

3.5     Optimization

Minimizing or maximizing a given quantity using a systematic method. Inclusion of geometric, physical, numeric, and map problems.

3.6     Applications of Differentiation in Economics

Law of Demand and demand functions. Introduction to revenue and cost functions, profit function, marginal cost functions, marginal revenue functions, and average cost functions. Maximizing revenue and profit.

3.7     Newton's Method

Newton's Method to approximate zeros of functions and solutions to equations. Discussion of when Newton's Method fails.
