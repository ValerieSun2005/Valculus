Chapter 4: Integration

4.1     Antidifferentiation

Definition of an antiderivative (indefinite integral). Exercises of basic antidifferentiation. Introduction to differential equations and solving initial-value problems. Application to position, velocity, and acceleration and objects in free fall.

4.2     Definite Integrals

Riemann definition of definite integral with geometric interpretation. Conversion between definite integrals and limits of Riemann sums. Evaluating Riemann sums algebraically and using tables. Properties of definite integrals.

4.3     Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, Part I and Part II, to evaluate definite integrals. Discussion of Chain Rule in Part I. Proof and geometric intuition.

4.4     Integration by Substitution

Transformation of an integral using a substitution. Discussion of changing of bounds and inclusion of Fundamental Theorem of Calculus. Antiderivatives of all six trigonometric functions. Integrals with odd and even functions by symmetry.

4.5     Numerical Integration

Left Riemann sums, right Riemann sums, and midpoint rules, with error bounds. Trapezoidal rule and error bound. Discussion of when each approach is an underestimate or overestimate. Derivation of Simpson's Rule, with error bound.
