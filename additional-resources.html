Additional Resources

Desmos An interactive graphing calculator that allows you to plot functions and their derivative graphs, as well as compute definite integrals and improper integrals. This resource allows to identify key features—zeros, intercepts, extrema, and asymptotes—of graphs.

Wolfram Alpha A website that performs complex mathematical operations, such as expanding functions, calculating zeros, taking derivatives and antiderivatives, and obtaining series representations of functions. It allows you to use natural language to fulfill a mathematical request—for example, differentiate x sin(x^2) or find the absolute maximum of x^3 - 4x + 6 on [1, 4].

Symbolab A solver that lists the steps to solve a complex problem, such as taking a derivative (including implicit differentiation) or integral.

Derivative Calculator A calculator that differentiates functions of any degree step by step. Because the algebra in differentiation can be complicated, it may be difficult to tell whether your answer equals our answer (or the calculator's answer). You can input your answer into the calculator, which will parse it and determine whether it is equivalent to the calculator's answer.

Integral Calculator A calculator that evaluates indefinite integrals and definite integrals, listing each step of the solving process. As you solve an integral, you may attain an answer that differs from our answer. This calculator allows you to enter your answer to check whether it equals, or differs by only a constant from, the calculator's answer (or our answer). For example, the calculator evaluates \(\int (2x - 1) \di x\) as \(x^2 - x + C.\) But \(\tfrac{1}{4} (2x - 1)^2 + C\) is also a correct answer; the calculator lets you know that both functions only differ by a constant, which makes both of them valid antiderivatives.

AP® Calculus AB Past Exam Questions and AP® Calculus BC Past Exam Questions Past free-response questions from the AP® Calculus exams. These problems, which contain solutions and scoring guidelines, are an excellent resource for exam preparation if you are enrolled in AP® Calculus. Even if you are not taking AP® Calculus, the questions are challenging enough to be beneficial for review. LaTeX Tutorial LaTeX is a programming language used to typeset documents, particularly ones with many mathematical equations. If you plan to pursue a mathematical field (including physics, engineering, or economics), then a knowledge of LaTeX is essential because it is the main tool for communicating mathematics. (The equations on this website are typeset using the syntax of LaTeX.)
